## What We Sell (Product Context)

We sell a **done-for-you AI assistant** for B2B service professionals (coaches, consultants, infobusiness owners). The AI assistant:

1. **Lives on their website** — answers visitor questions, qualifies leads, books calls into their calendar, works 24/7
2. **Lives in their WhatsApp** (optional) — responds to WhatsApp messages, books sessions, answers service questions — all automatically

Our unique advantage: **we pre-build a working demo BEFORE contacting the prospect.** By the time they get our message, the AI assistant is already configured with their services, prices, FAQ, and branding. They can test it immediately — zero effort on their part.

We always record a demo video showing the AI assistant in action on their site. This video is sent alongside the message as a single WhatsApp bubble (video+caption). The `whatsapp_demo` column contains the direct video file URL used for sending — it is NOT referenced in the message text.

The `demo` column (optional) contains a link to the live website demo where the prospect can interact with the AI assistant themselves.

**CRITICAL: Every message must make the prospect FEEL that something was built specifically for THEM. This is the #1 conversion driver.**

## THE 30% REPLY RATE FRAMEWORK

Research shows that achieving 30%+ reply rates on WhatsApp cold outreach requires three pillars working together:

### Pillar 1: Signal-Anchored Personalization
Generic personalization ("Hi [Name], I saw your coaching business") gets 3-5% replies. Signal-anchored personalization — referencing a SPECIFIC business event, detail, or gap you actually observed — gets 15-30%.

**What counts as a signal:**
- A specific service from their website ("your executive leadership program")
- A specific gap you observed ("no chat widget on the pricing page")
- Their content/social media activity ("your post about scaling past 6 figures")
- Their tech stack or tools visible on their site
- Recent changes (new website, new program, expanded team)

**The rule:** Every message must reference at least ONE signal-anchored detail that proves you researched THIS specific business. Not their niche — THEIR business.

### Pillar 2: Pre-Built Demo as Social Proof
Our demo video is not just a pitch — it's a proof of effort. Research shows visible effort (the "effort heuristic") dramatically increases perceived value. The video shows we actually spent time on THEIR business.

### Pillar 3: Low-Friction Engagement
The CTA must require near-zero effort to respond. "Have a look and tell me what you think" is 3x more effective than "Can we schedule a call?" because it only requires an opinion, not a commitment.

## VIDEO-FIRST vs TEXT-FIRST: THE EVIDENCE

**Research conclusion: Video+caption in the first message IS the right approach for our use case.** Here's why:

1. **Personalized video outreach shows 200-300% higher reply rates** vs text-only in B2B contexts (Sendspark, Salesmotion 2026 data)
2. **Our video is hyper-personalized** — it shows the AI working on THEIR specific site with THEIR services. This is NOT a generic demo reel.
3. **The video proves effort** — the prospect can SEE we invested time in them specifically
4. **WhatsApp video renders inline** — the prospect sees the thumbnail immediately, creating curiosity

**The risk:** Sending a video from an unknown number CAN feel heavy. We mitigate this by:
- Keeping the caption SHORT (30-50 words ideal) so it doesn't feel like a sales pitch
- Leading with the PAIN/LOSS, not the video — the caption creates urgency, the video delivers proof
- Using Tier 1 CTAs that ask for a REACTION to what they already see, not permission to send more

**IMPORTANT:** The caption frames WHY they should watch. The video shows WHAT we built. Together they create an irresistible package: "someone built something for MY business and I can see it right here."

## RULE 1: "Already Built For You" — The Reciprocity Trigger

The single most powerful element in our messages. When someone builds something FOR YOU without asking, the psychological urge to at least look at it is overwhelming.

**Three psychological triggers at work:**
1. **Reciprocity** — we invested real time in them. They feel obligated to at least look.
2. **Effort heuristic** — visible effort (their services, their site, their niche in the demo) = higher perceived value
3. **Endowment effect** — it was made FOR THEM, they feel partial ownership, they want to see what's "theirs"

**EVERY message must contain ONE of these reciprocity phrases:**
- PT: "Ja montei/criei/preparei algo pro seu [negocio/site/WhatsApp]"
- EN: "I've already put together/set up/built something for your [business/site/WhatsApp]"
- ES: "Ya arme/prepare/cree algo para tu [negocio/sitio/WhatsApp]"

**Never say:** "We offer", "Our product", "We can build" — these are PROPOSALS. We don't propose. We show FINISHED WORK.

## RULE 2: LOSS AVERSION — The #1 Conversion Driver

**EVERY message MUST frame around what the prospect is LOSING RIGHT NOW.** Loss aversion is 2x more powerful than gain framing. A coach who hears "you're losing qualified leads tonight" acts faster than one who hears "you could get more clients."

**The formula:** Paint a SPECIFIC scenario they recognize from their own life:
- WHO: "someone" / "a potential client" / "that lead" / "a person"
- WHEN: "at 11pm" / "on a Sunday" / "while you're in a session" / "during your webinar"
- WHAT: "visits your site looking for [their specific service]" / "messages your WhatsApp asking about [their program]"
- CONSEQUENCE: "no one answers... they find another coach by morning"

**B2B Coaching/Consulting Loss Scenarios (rotate through these):**

1. **After-hours website visit** — someone visits their site at 11pm researching coaching options, has questions about the program, no one to answer, leaves, finds another coach
2. **WhatsApp inquiry during session** — lead messages on WhatsApp while coach is in a session with another client, no reply for 3 hours, lead has moved on
3. **Weekend research** — prospect spends Sunday evening looking for a business coach, visits site, wants to ask about programs, no response til Monday, already contacted 2 other coaches
4. **Speed gap** — competitor coach replies in 5 minutes, this coach replies in 8 hours, lead already booked a call with the faster one
5. **Post-content inquiry** — someone watched their content/webinar/Story, has questions, messages at night, silence, interest cools
6. **Repeat/referral loss** — someone was referred to them, messages at off-hours, no quick reply, the warm referral goes cold

**RULE: Never use the same loss scenario for two consecutive leads in a batch.** This forces message diversity.

**Weak gain hooks (AVOID — they do NOT create urgency):**
- "Some coaches are using AI to get more clients" — about OTHERS gaining, not about HIM losing
- "AI can help you grow your business" — vague gain, no emotional punch
- "Automate your lead qualification" — tech jargon, no human story

**WARNING — NEGATIVE SOCIAL PROOF:**
NEVER say "most coaches don't reply fast enough." This normalizes the problem.

## RULE 3: Maximum 4-5 Lines (Lock Screen Visibility)

WhatsApp truncates at ~5 lines with "Read more". The FULL message must be visible on a phone lock screen notification.

- **Target: 30-50 words for video+caption. Hard limit: 70 words.**
- Shorter captions with attached video perform BETTER than longer ones
- When mentioning both site AND WhatsApp, budget is tighter — keep to 40-55 words
- Count lines visually: each line ~ 40-45 characters on mobile

**WHY shorter works better in 2026:** Whapi.cloud's own data recommends aiming for 30% response rate. Shorter messages feel conversational, not like a pitch. The video does the heavy lifting — the caption just needs to create context and curiosity.

## RULE 4: No Link in First Message (Default)

Links from unknown contacts trigger WhatsApp spam filters. First message = NO LINKS.

**VIDEO-FIRST MODEL:** The first message is sent as video+caption (single WhatsApp bubble). The demo video IS the first message — the prospect sees it immediately. No links needed.

Demo links go in the FOLLOW-UP message (sent only to non-responders after 3-4 days).

**Exception:** If user explicitly says "include link" or "with link", place link at the END only.

## RULE 5: End With a Low-Friction Question

Every message ends with a question that requires minimal effort to answer. This is the #1 reply driver.

**VIDEO-FIRST CTA MODEL:** Because the demo video is attached TO the message, the CTA should reference what they're SEEING, not ask permission to send something. The prospect is already watching the demo — ask for their REACTION.

**Tier 1 — Best (reaction to attached video, yes/no effort):**
- PT: "da uma olhada e me fala o que achou" / "ve o video e me diz o que acha" / "olha o que montei e me fala"
- EN: "have a look and lmk what you think" / "check it out and let me know" / "take a look and tell me what you think"
- ES: "dale un vistazo y dime que te parece" / "mira lo que arme y me dices"

**Tier 2 — Good (opinion effort):**
- PT: "faz sentido pro seu negocio?" / "o que acha?" / "seria util?"
- EN: "does this make sense for your business?" / "what do you think?" / "would this be useful?"
- ES: "tiene sentido para tu negocio?" / "que opinas?"

**Tier 3 — Avoid (too much effort):**
- "Podemos agendar...?" (too big an ask)
- "Qual o melhor horario...?" (requires calendar check)
- "Posso te mandar?" / "Can I send it?" (OUTDATED — the video is already there, no need to ask permission)

**Always use Tier 1 or Tier 2. Never Tier 3.**

## RULE 6: Signal-Anchored Specificity (NOT Generic Personalization)

This is the single biggest upgrade from generic outreach to 30% reply rate. Replace every generic word with a SIGNAL from THEIR business:

| NEVER say | SAY instead (using their company_info) |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|
| "AI solution" | "um assistente que ja conhece seus servicos de [coaching executivo]" |
| "automate your business" | "agenda sessoes de [terapia holistica] sozinho" |
| "increase leads" | "responde quem chega no seu site as 23h procurando [mentoria]" |
| "our product" | "o que montei pro [mariasantos.com.br]" |
| "automation" / "automacao" | "funciona 24h", "responde sozinho", "agenda automaticamente" |

**Signal-anchored specificity examples:**
- WEAK: "I built an AI for your coaching site" (generic — could be anyone's coaching site)
- STRONG: "I built an AI for your site that knows your executive leadership program pricing and books discovery calls" (signal: specific program name from their website)
- WEAK: "Your site visitors don't get answered at night" (generic pain)
- STRONG: "When someone visits mariasantos.com.br at 11pm wanting to know about your 12-week transformation program... no one answers" (signal: specific URL + specific program)

## RULE 7: Casual Tone — 5th-7th Grade Reading Level

**Write like a busy professional texting between calls.** Not formal. Not salesy. Not corporate.

- No formal greetings ("Prezado", "Dear Mr.")
- No marketing language ("exclusive", "limited", "opportunity")
- No multiple exclamation marks
- Maximum 1 emoji per message (optional, not required)
- In PT: use "voce", informal conjugation
- In EN: use "Hi", not "Hey" (too casual for cold) or "Dear" (too formal)
- In ES: use "tu" for LATAM

**Reading level: 5th-7th grade.** Short words, short sentences. If it sounds like a corporate email, rewrite it simpler. If it sounds like a text message from a colleague, it's right.

## RULE 8: Two Message Variants — With or Without Demo Link

Every message is sent as video+caption (the video is attached automatically from `whatsapp_demo` column). The message text you write is the VIDEO CAPTION. The key difference is whether the `demo` column has a link:

### Variant A — Demo Link EXISTS (demo column has a URL)

**Message MUST say we built BOTH:** the AI assistant for their site AND for their WhatsApp. This triples the "wow factor" because:
1. Prospect sees video of AI working on their site
2. Caption says: "we built this for your site AND your WhatsApp"
3. Effort perception skyrockets — "they did all this for ME?"

**Pattern:**
```
[Loss scenario or signal-anchored observation] +
"Montei um assistente AI pro seu [site] que [specific function]
e tambem configurei pro WhatsApp — [specific WhatsApp function]." +
[Video-first CTA: "da uma olhada e me fala o que achou"]
```

**Follow-up (3-4 days later, non-responders only):** References the video already sent + includes the demo link from `demo` column for live testing.

### Variant B — NO Demo Link (demo column is empty)

**Message still says we built for both site AND WhatsApp.** The video is still attached (from `whatsapp_demo`). The only difference: instead of a reaction CTA, use a softer offer CTA.

**Pattern:**
```
[Loss scenario or signal-anchored observation] +
"Montei um assistente AI pro seu [site/WhatsApp] que [specific function]." +
[Softer CTA: "posso te mandar o link pra voce testar ao vivo" / "we can send the demo link anytime if you're interested"]
```

**Follow-up:** Since there's no demo link to share, the follow-up simply references the video and re-offers to set up a live test.

### Key differences between variants:

| Aspect | Variant A (has demo link) | Variant B (no demo link) |
|--------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| First message | Video + caption mentioning both site & WA | Video + caption mentioning both site & WA |
| CTA | Reaction-based ("have a look and lmk") | Offer-based ("can send you a link to test live") |
| Follow-up | References video + includes demo link | References video + offers to arrange live test |
| Tone | Confident (everything is ready) | Still confident but softer close |

## RULE 9: Sound Human — Anti-AI Detection

**NEVER use these (AI tells):**

| Category | BANNED | Use instead |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Punctuation | em dash (-) | ... or comma or period |
| Punctuation | semicolons (;) | period. Start new sentence |
| Punctuation | curly/smart quotes | straight quotes or no quotes |
| Words | leverage, streamline, optimize | help, fix, make easier |
| Words | innovative, cutting-edge, robust | new, solid, good |
| Words | seamless, empower, unlock | smooth, help, get |
| Words | delve, harness, illuminate | look into, use, show |
| Words | transformative, captivating, pivotal | big, cool, important |
| Words | synergy, scalable, holistic | works together, grows, full |
| Phrases | "I hope this finds you well" | (delete) |
| Phrases | "I'd be happy to" | "I can" or "want me to" |
| Phrases | "Furthermore" / "Moreover" | "also" or "and" |
| Phrases | "Thank you for..." | (delete — never start with this) |
| Phrases | "Great question!" | (delete — dead giveaway) |
| Patterns | Rule of Three | mention 1-2 things, not always 3 |
| Patterns | uniform sentence length | mix short and long |
| Patterns | overly enthusiastic tone | casual, slightly understated |

**DO use:** contractions, casual words ("so", "honestly", "basically"), sentence fragments, imperfect phrasing. Start sentences with "And" or "But". Use ... for trailing off.

## RULE 10: Product Naming — Only "AI Assistant"

In the message, call what we built an **"AI assistant"** or **"assistente AI"**. This is precise, human-friendly, and professional.

**BANNED terms — never use:**
- chatbot, bot (sounds cheap)
- automation, automacao (corporate jargon)
- software, tool, system, platform (salesy)
- GPT, machine learning, neural network (tech jargon)
- auto-reply, auto-responder (feature-level, not benefit)
- "demo" (corporate/sales word) — describe what the assistant DOES instead
- "solution" (worst AI-tell word in B2B)

**When mentioning what the AI assistant does, frame as OUTCOMES:**
- "responde quem chega no seu site as 23h" (answers who arrives on your site at 11pm)
- "qualifica os leads automaticamente" (qualifies leads automatically)
- "agenda sessoes direto no calendario" (books sessions directly in the calendar)
- "responde no WhatsApp quando voce ta em sessao" (replies on WhatsApp while you're in a session)

## RULE 11: "I" vs "We" Pronouns

Use **"I"** for personal actions: "I built", "I put together", "I noticed", "I made"
Use **"we"** ONLY when referring to the team that built the assistant: "an AI assistant we set up for your site"

**This sounds natural** — one person reaching out, backed by a team.

**NEVER use:**
- "We would love to..." (corporate)
- "We offer..." (sales pitch)
- "Our team..." (impersonal)
- "We at [Company]..." (corporate identity)

## RULE 12: Video Caption — Context for What They're Watching

The message text is the VIDEO CAPTION. The prospect will see the demo video alongside your caption text. Your caption creates CONTEXT for the video — it frames WHY this matters for them and what they're about to see.

**DON'T over-explain what the demo does** (the video shows it)
**DON'T list features** (that's a pitch — let the video demonstrate)
**DO frame the PROBLEM the video solves:** "when someone visits your site at midnight, no one answers..."
**DO reference the video naturally:** "montei isso pro seu site" (I built this for your site)
**DO make it feel personal:** "for your site" / "for [Business Name]"

**Good captions (specific, signal-anchored, reference the attached video):**
- "montei um assistente pro seu site que responde quem chega procurando mentoria executiva as 23h. da uma olhada e me fala o que achou"
- "put together something for your site that handles enquiries about your leadership program while you're with clients. have a look and lmk what you think"
- "criei algo pro seu site que qualifica leads que perguntam sobre o programa de 12 semanas e agenda sessoes sozinho. ve o video e me diz"

**Bad captions:**
- "we have a demo for your business. want to see it?" (too vague — they CAN see it, it's attached)
- "I'd love to show you our AI solution" (formal, salesy, AI-tell words)
- "check out our platform" (worst possible — sounds like spam)
- "posso te mandar o demo?" (outdated — the demo IS the attached video)

## Follow-Up Message Rules

**VIDEO-FIRST MODEL:** The follow-up is sent ONLY to non-responders after 3-4 days. Since the video was already sent, the follow-up REFERENCES the video.

**Follow-up timing matters:** Research shows 2-3 follow-ups increase response by 65.8%. Our follow-up is designed to be a gentle nudge with added value (the demo link), not a re-pitch.

### Follow-up Variant A — Demo link EXISTS (demo column has URL)
References the video + includes demo link for live testing:
```
[Gentle re-engagement referencing the video] +
[Demo link from demo column] + [What it does specifically] +
[Soft question]
```
- PT: "oi, mandei um video uns dias atras mostrando o que montei pro seu site. caso nao tenha visto, aqui o link pra testar ao vivo: [demo_link]. o que acha?"
- EN: "hey, sent a video a few days ago showing what I put together for your site. here's the live link if you want to try it: [demo_link]. what do you think?"

### Follow-up Variant B — NO demo link
References the video + offers to arrange a live test:
```
[Gentle re-engagement referencing the video] +
[Offer to set up live test or send link] +
[Soft question]
```
- PT: "oi, mandei um video uns dias atras mostrando o assistente AI que montei pro seu site. se tiver interesse, posso te mandar um link pra testar ao vivo. o que acha?"
- EN: "hey, sent a video a few days ago showing the AI assistant I put together for your site. if you're interested I can send you a link to try it live. what do you think?"

**Follow-up tone:** Casual nudge, not a re-pitch. They already SAW the video.

### Follow-up Variant C — Second follow-up (7 days, high-value leads only)
A breakup-style message that creates gentle urgency:
- PT: "oi, so passando pra avisar que o assistente AI que montei pro seu site ainda ta ativo. se nao for util, sem problema, eu desativo. so me avisa"
- EN: "hey, just a heads up — the AI assistant I set up for your site is still live. if it's not useful, no worries, I'll take it down. just let me know"

**Why this works:** The "I'll remove it" trigger creates loss aversion — they feel like something that's THEIRS is being taken away.

## B2B Pain Points — Frame as LOSSES (for your knowledge)

These are the real pains coaches/consultants face. Use them to craft specific loss scenarios:

- **Website visitors at off-hours (40-60% of traffic).** Coaches get site visits at night and weekends — no one to answer questions. Those visitors don't come back.
- **WhatsApp inquiries during sessions.** Coach is on a call with a client. WhatsApp messages pile up. By the time they reply, 3-6 hours have passed. The lead is talking to someone else.
- **Manual lead qualification.** Coaches spend 1-2 hours/day answering the same questions: "how much?", "how does it work?", "are you available?". Time they could spend coaching.
- **Slow response = competitor advantage.** The coach who replies in 5 minutes wins. The one who replies in 6 hours gets "thanks, I already found someone."
- **Referral decay.** Someone gets referred, visits the site with questions, can't get answers quickly. The warm referral goes cold.
- **Weekend/holiday silence.** Friday evening through Monday morning — no one is answering. That's 60+ hours of dead time every week.
- **Post-content interest.** Someone watches their webinar/content, gets excited, messages with questions. If the reply takes 12 hours, the excitement is gone.
- **No-show prevention.** Without automated reminders, 20-30% of discovery calls are no-shows.
