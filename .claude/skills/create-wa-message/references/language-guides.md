# Language-Specific Guidelines

## Portuguese (Brazil) — "pt"

- Informal "voce", never "o senhor"
- Brazilian expressions: "Oi", "E ai", "Show!", "Tudo bem?"
- Best CTAs: "da uma olhada e me fala o que achou" / "ve o video e me diz o que acha"
- Natural WhatsApp spelling — not formal written Portuguese
- Reference Pix, WhatsApp culture (98.9% penetration)
- Coaching terms in PT: "sessao", "mentorado", "aluno", "processo", "agendamento"

### Brazilian cultural notes for higher reply rates
- Brazilians prefer warmth and relationship before business — a quick "Oi" opener feels natural
- WhatsApp is THE communication app in Brazil — 98.9% smartphone penetration
- Avoid overly formal language — even professionals text casually
- Mentioning "site" and "WhatsApp" together hits a nerve: every coach KNOWS they lose WhatsApp leads
- Time references that resonate: "23h" (late night), "fim de semana" (weekend), "durante uma sessao"

## English (Ireland/UK) — "en"

- Warm but slightly more formal than PT
- "Hi" not "Hey" (first contact)
- Use "enquiries", "bookings", "diary" (not "appointments", "schedule")
- Best CTAs: "have a look and lmk what you think" / "check it out and let me know"
- Don't over-sell — understatement works better in Irish culture
- Irish humor is self-deprecating — never be overly enthusiastic

### Irish/UK cultural notes
- Subtlety wins. "Put something together" sounds better than "built an AI system"
- They'll be skeptical of big claims — focus on the specific thing you built, not outcomes
- "Worth a quick look?" is a culturally perfect CTA for Ireland/UK

## Spanish (LATAM — Colombia, Mexico) — "es"

- Informal "tu" for most LATAM countries
- "Hola", "Que tal", "Oye"
- Best CTAs: "dale un vistazo y dime que te parece" / "mira lo que arme y me dices"
- Use country-specific terms if city is known

### LATAM cultural notes
- Colombia: slightly more formal than Mexico, "usted" in some regions
- Mexico: casual "tu", WhatsApp is dominant but not quite Brazil-level
- Both countries: mentioning "WhatsApp" as a business tool resonates strongly
- Time references: "a las 11pm", "un domingo", "mientras estas con un cliente"
